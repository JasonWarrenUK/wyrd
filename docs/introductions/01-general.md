# Wyrd: What It Is

*For anyone curious, not just developers.*

## The Problem

Productivity tools assume you have one type of work. You don't. You have projects competing for attention, promises made to people that slip through the cracks, ideas that arrive mid-task and derail focus, routines you keep dropping, and notes scattered across five different apps.

Existing tools address a subset of this and reproduce the rest. A to-do app doesn't know what blocks what. A calendar doesn't know you've been neglecting a project for two weeks. A journal app doesn't connect your reflections to the tasks that prompted them.

## What Wyrd Does

Wyrd is a personal system that connects everything: tasks, notes, journal entries, commitments, habits, and budgets. They all live in the same place, and they're all linked to each other.

It has three governing principles.

**It shows you consequences, never nags you.** If you spend too long on one task, the rest of your day visibly compresses. If you overspend a budget, the progress bar turns red. The system makes costs visible; you make the decisions.

**It adapts to you.** You define your own task types, your own daily rituals, your own views into your data. The system provides the structure; you decide what goes in it.

**It works offline.** Everything runs on your machine. No accounts, no cloud dependency. Optional AI features (task suggestions, journal analysis) enhance the system but aren't required.

## What It Looks Like

Wyrd runs in the terminal. A split-screen view shows your day's schedule on the left and detail on the right. A capture bar at the bottom lets you jot down a thought without leaving your current task. Everything is keyboard-driven.

Four visual themes ship with the app: two dark (Ember & Violet; Peat & Teal) and two light (Kiln; Ordnance Survey).

## The Name

Wyrd is an Anglo-Saxon word meaning fate, or more precisely, "that which has become." It captures the idea that your present situation is shaped by past choices. The app makes that visible: here's what you chose, here's what it cost, here's where you are now.
